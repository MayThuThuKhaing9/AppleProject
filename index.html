The Garage-to-iPhone Transformation

Our history is more than a timeline of products; it is the ultimate Silicon Valley saga of vision, adversity, and world-changing design. It begins in 1976, born from the collaboration of our founders in a suburban California garage, fueled by a singular desire to put computing power into the hands of everyday people.

Our early triumphs, the Apple II and the revolutionary Macintosh in 1984, established our foundational ethos: technology must be intuitive and beautiful. We pioneered the graphical user interface and the mouse to make computing personal and accessible.

Following a challenging period, the return of our co-founder in 1997 catalyzed a dramatic, sustained turnaround. Our second chapter was defined by a relentless focus on simplicity and aesthetic clarity, giving rise to iconic devices like the colorful iMac and the culture-defining iPod, which changed the way the world consumed music.

The ultimate breakthrough arrived in 2007 with the launch of the iPhone. This singular product was the culmination of three decades of lessons in hardware, software, and industrial design, fundamentally redefining the personal technology experience. By combining the power of our Mac operating system with the convenience of iPod and adding mobile connectivity, we transitioned from being a computer maker to a global arbiter of digital life, forever setting the standard for user experience and innovation.
